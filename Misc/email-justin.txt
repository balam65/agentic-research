Moving towards a maximally agentic paradigm, my most immediate suggestion would be to start by specifying things in terms of only non-negotiable inputs and outputs, rather than in terms of process flow. This *might* include seemingly "intermediate" outputs, but only if they have indispensible value in their own right.

So, for example, it's worth considering whether "structured research brief" is a necessary fundamental output: if you're mandating that you want human oversight injected at that point, then it may well be; but if it exists only to encourage the agent to approach things in a structured way, then it shouldn't be part of the top-level specification. You can always layer on additional recommended structure (and some of this may end up getting promoted to mandated structure). but its important to start by clearly outlining the absolute non-negotiables.

There's a parallel here that you will appreciate with feature engineering in data science and machine learning: we want to keep the feature engineering to an absolute minimum, and give the AI the opportunity to do as much of the work as possible. If we find that it struggles to give us what we want, then we can "nudge" it by injecting some additional inductive biases.
